Why visit Barcelona?

Source: this text uses content retrieved from the Spain Holiday and Wikipedia.

Barcelona is a major cultural and economic centre in southwestern Europe, being Spain’s second largest and definitely most cosmopolitan city, and one of the Mediterranean’s busiest ports. It is the capital of Catalonia, which is a region of Spain with its own language – Catalan –, and it’s own culture and character.

Barcelona offers the best of both worlds, situated on the Costa Brava on the Spanish Mediterranean coast, the city gently rises up from the coastline through the city and up into a range of wooded hills.

Barcelona is a dynamic, open Mediterranean city. It’s the perfect city to relax, stroll around and enjoy its unique way of life.

In terms of culture and art, there is a lot to see; it is known to always have something new and interesting awaiting around every corner: Gothic architecture, the famous Las Ramblas avenue, Gaudi’s Güel Parc and the unfinished La Sagrada Familia Cathedral, the old Raval quarter, La Ribera and Barceloneta, a beachside ravel of corridor style streets where you can find some fantastic local restaurants and bars, away from the typical tourist area.

Getting around Barcelona is easy. You can pretty much walk around the whole city, but they also have a daily rent-a-bike system and the public transport by Metro and bus is very good and simple to use. For more information, check the Transport information.

Famous for breaking ground art, architecture and avant-garde chefs; the city eclipses many other for its creativity and forward thinking.
